## Executive Summary

Acme is in their fourth bi-weekly cycle and the relationship is on a strong upward trajectory. Since last call, we shipped Project Templates (91 projects created from templates since launch), reduced generation latency by 18%, and surfaced the early CI/CD integration scaffolding behind a flag. Their VP of Eng opened with strong enthusiasm: *"We are digging it. It's off to a great start and it's something that we've been looking forward to having for so long." — Their VP of Eng*

This call focused on three areas: (1) shared template UX issues, (2) team workspaces and access controls, and (3) deployment visibility within Forge.

**Templates for Batch Projects.** Acme is using templates heavily for compliance-sensitive batch projects but flagged five concrete fast-follows: template versioning when source templates update, misleading "generated by" metadata on hand-customized templates, missing failed-generations visibility, missing deployment-environment indicators, and a missing "view deployment" affordance from generated project cards. We broke these down into Linear tickets in sprint planning and assigned to Sam and Jordan this week.

**Team Workspaces and Access Controls.** Their reaction to the access-permissions preview was immediate: *"You had me at hello, Hannah. You had me at hello." — Their VP of Eng*. They're already assigning internal owners to develop a rollout plan before the feature ships.

**Deployment Visibility.** Their Head of Product flagged that they have to leave Forge and check the AWS console to see deploy status — *"There is a high chance that if it's a project deployed through Forge Teams, it's already running on our infrastructure. So we're not spinning up duplicate instances." — Their Head of Product*

**How Acme is Using Templates and Generation Deflection.** They're using Forge templates to enforce compliance patterns for regulated batch projects. They generate the project, customize, and deploy through their internal CI/CD before the work reaches production. The template-to-deploy conversion rate we're seeing for Acme (40%) is the floor; they expect it to climb materially once CI/CD integration ships.

**Opportunity Areas.** Three areas to lean into: (1) treat the five template fast-follows as a compounding wedge — every fix increases trust and unlocks more projects per cycle; (2) prioritize Team Workspaces, since Acme is the prototypical customer for it and unprompted internal staffing is a strong buying signal; (3) get deployment visibility inside Forge in time for their Q1 rollout — this is a known long-standing ask across multiple customers.

**Forge's Upcoming Roadmap Validates User-Expansion Strategy.** Acme's reaction validates our hypothesis that Access Controls + Team Workspaces shifts Forge from an individual prototyping tool to a team-wide dev platform. The signal is strongest when customers assign internal owners *before the feature ships* — Acme has done that.

**Key Product Gaps**
- **Template versioning:** Updates to a template orphan projects already created from it. Acme is currently locking template versions manually as a workaround.
- **Failed-generation visibility:** No surface for which template configs are producing low-quality output. Their VP is grepping logs.
- **Deploy status in Forge:** Their team is bouncing to AWS console to check deploy state, leading to duplicate environment provisioning.
- **CI/CD integration:** Implied gap — they're manually exporting generated code and re-importing into their CI pipeline. Implies Forge needs direct CI/CD integration.

## Insights / Learnings

### Template Adoption and UX

Templates are landing well as a compliance-and-speed wedge, but the feature has clear UX rough edges that hurt trust on every iteration cycle.

| Insight | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Template versioning is the #1 issue** | When a template updates after projects have been created from it, those projects are orphaned — they don't pick up the new template version, and there's no migration path. Acme is locking template versions manually as a workaround.<br><br>*"We've started just freezing the templates because we can't trust the upgrade path yet." — Their VP of Eng* |
| **"Generated by Forge" metadata feels misleading on customized templates** | Once a customer hand-customizes a template, the metadata still claims it's Forge-generated. Internally, this confuses their reviewers — they assume no human has touched it.<br><br>*"It looks like the AI did everything, even though we've been editing it for two weeks." — Their Head of Product* |

### Team Workspaces as an Expansion Vector

Team Workspaces is the single feature most likely to convert Acme from individual-tool usage to team-wide adoption.

| Insight | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Customer is staffing the rollout before the feature ships** | Their VP has already assigned two team members to develop an internal rollout plan, including a weekly review process where engineering leads sign off on team projects.<br><br>*"What would really be helpful would be like if you guys created some sort of process where our engineering leads had to sign in once a week and review their team's projects." — Their VP of Eng* |
