# Buyer Persona: Example Buyer

| | |
|---|---|
| **Title** | VP Engineering at $5M-$50M ARR SaaS |
| **Reports to** | CTO or CEO |
| **Decision authority** | Can sign $50K, needs CFO for $50K+ |
| **Buying timeline** | 30-90 days |

## What They Care About

- Shipping reliable software on time
- Their team's productivity and morale
- Not getting paged at 2 AM
- Their reputation with the CTO/CEO (will this work make me look smart?)

## What They Fear

- Tools that demo well but break in production
- Becoming the person who chose the failed vendor
- Procurement / security review blocking the deal
- Lock-in to a tool the team will hate in 6 months

## How They Evaluate

- They will trial before buying. Make trials successful.
- They will ask their team. Make sure your champion isn't isolated.
- They will check Reddit/Hacker News for horror stories. Have answers ready.
- They will ask for references. Have 2-3 named permissioned customers in `customers/case-studies/`.

## Signals They're Ready to Buy

- They invite their security team to the next call
- They ask about contract terms (annual vs. monthly)
- They forward your one-pager to their CFO unprompted
- They ask "what does the rollout look like?"

## Signals They're Stalling

- They keep asking the same questions twice
- Their champion stops responding to your emails
- They want a custom demo for "one more stakeholder" (you're being shopped)

## The Sentences That Land

- "Your team can be running this in 30 minutes — let me show you on the call"
- "We have [N] customers at your scale; here are their renewal rates"
- "This is what your runbook looks like at 2 AM" (show our docs)

## The Sentences That Don't

- "It's enterprise-grade" (meaningless)
- "We integrate with everything" (tell me about *my* stack)
- "Other customers love it" (which? when? why?)

## Internal Reference

Update this persona after every 5 deals — wins or losses. Capture a one-line "what changed in their buying behavior" note in `customers/win-loss/`.
