| **When you'll see them** | Mid-market deals, 50-200 seat range; deals where buyer mentions "fast prototyping" |
| **Their core pitch** | "Ship apps in days, not months" — speed-first, design-quality-second |
| **Their weaknesses** | No enterprise auth, no audit logging, no role-based access, weak deploy story |

## Their Strengths (Don't Try to Out-Pitch These)

- Time-to-first-deploy: ~2 hours vs. our ~30 min target
- Brand recognition in YC / SF startup scene
- Free tier with no card required

## Our Counter

| Their Pitch | Our Response | Proof |
|-------------|-------------|-------|
| "We're faster" | We're production-ready. Speed without security/audit is a deal-blocker at the buyer's review stage. | `customers/case-studies/{customer}-procurement-blocked-by-x.md` |
| "We have more templates" | Templates are commodity; what matters is *what compiles to production*. Show our deploy success rate. | `analytics/dashboards/billing/credit-usage-dashboards.md` (deploy success ~92% vs theirs ~73%) |
| "Our pricing is simpler" | Their per-seat model breaks at 50+ users (3x our cost at scale). | `decisions/2026-02-14-usage-based-pricing.md` |

## Traps to Avoid

- **Don't compete on speed alone.** They win that frame.
- **Don't badmouth them on Twitter.** Their CEO retweets it and you become the story.
- **Don't promise our enterprise features at SMB pricing.** Renewal pain.

## When We Lose to Them

Common pattern: buyer is a single founder, not a team. They genuinely don't need our enterprise features yet. Acceptable loss; track for re-engagement at series A.

## When They Lose to Us

Common pattern: their procurement blocks them at the security/audit review. Win condition: get to procurement early, attach our SOC2 + audit logging early in the cycle.
